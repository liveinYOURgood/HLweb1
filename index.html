    Native Strength

    Native Strength is a centuries-old inter-tribal traditional mental health care system. It uses medicine wheels, teaching triangles, and traditional stories to illustrate concepts and provide useful tools for personal insight and coping strategies.

    Getting to Know Hyapatia Lee

    Hyapatia Lee is the founder of Native Strength. After experiencing a successful traditional ceremony to have a baby, Hyapatia began studying with two medicine men, a medicine woman (Gladys Tantaquideon, brother to the last chief of the Mohicans), and a Hawaiian Kahuna. These teachers shared traditional wisdom from the Mayan, Lakota, Cherokee, Hawaiian, and many other cultures.

    In 2001 Hyapatia was elected to the prestigious position of Blessed Woman for the Lost River band of the Cherokee. Every weekend she taught the people to speak Cherokee, conducted pipe ceremonies and presided over sweat lodges, healing ceremonies. As Hypatia began to teach the many things she had learned from the elders, it became apparent that this information was just as useful to the members of the Lost River band as it had been to her.

    Medicine Wheels

    Few of us escape childhood unscathed and Hyapatia is no exception. With the help of the medicine Wheels (Some examples above and below), we can heal from that past. The tools on the Native Strength path will show us how to connect with our inner children and wise elders. Our inner elders can give us the love, acceptance, and understanding we deserve and have been missing. They can show us paths to happiness and success we may have not noticed before.

    Medicine Wheels help us to remember and comprehend this complicated system. This collection became known as Native Strength as it awakens the strength that is inside each of us. Please click course button below to learn more about this with this interesting class offered.
